I am 25, soon to be 26, years old. I have a BSc in Physics, a MSc in Computer Science. I have worked for my university in IT, which I followed by working for someone that I later found out that he was a criminal and lastly as a QA engineer for the past three years. I enrolled in the army a year ago, and with me de-listing in a few weeks there has never been a time in my life where there was so much potential about the future. The only thing that stands between me and my ambitions is work, hard work.

Right now I am building an application to solve one of my biggest enemies when being a QA engineer. As much as I loved the analysis, and the testing and the whole point of stretching a piece of machinery to its absolute limits, I also had to endure the process of writing test cases. 

Fundamentally, in QA and UAT, test cases express the ways in which the user may interact with your product. You need to come with scenarios by placing yourself in the shoes of the end user. You need to play the act from the POV of a good and a bad actor. What would your ideal customer want from their experience with your product, and how would someone attempt to exploit it? Truly fascinating stuff. 

When it comes to documenting all of these scenarios, it is a very tedious process. Having a test basis is a true asset for any team that builds a product, maintaining that database, being consistent and future-proof are very difficult goals. The whole process, done right, involves constant hands-on work, following strict guidelines and effective use of previous work. 

The name of the project is TRG (Test Rag Generation) and its main goal is to help people like me do our work more efficiently. By fitting neatly into the existing workflow of QA engineers, it lowers the friction required when writing test cases by taking care of the boring stuff.

I've fallen into the trap many times before. I've failed to deliver projects, even half-baked ones due to fear. I've read the books and listened to the people who have done it. This time, I am getting this to the hands of QA people no matter what, and I am documenting my journey.
