---
title: Blog_006 - Overwhelming stimulation
date: 2026-06-20
---

Got into the vicious cycle of wanting to do many things at one and ending up doing nothing. Can't feel satisfied, and that's the worst part. I have the habit of opening YouTube and or Instagram every single time in bored. As a man in my 20s my algorithm is filled with inspirational stuff. Highlights of other people's lives. New shiny things. Shredded people. High achievers. Beautiful people. Aesthetic places. Over complicated and very tasty looking food. Famous people. Successful people. Truth is that my life is so disconnected from theirs that I'll probably never experience what they are. 

Then, there are the products. Carefully crafted marketing campaigns that land on my screen. I tend to like more aesthetic and premium looking stuff and it did not take the algorithm too long to figure that out. Truth is that I'm nowhere near their target demographic, simply because I do not have enough money to spent on these things. The reality of my disposable income is disconnected from my taste and to be honest I don't need that many things anyway. 

I'm sure that what fuels these two very distinct categories to show up is my underlying desire to possess what they are portraying. I do want the lifestyle and the products, but I'm not yet able to obtain them. It's frustrating but it is what reality is. 

Truth is that I have the tools I need to escape. It's just so damn easier to scroll rather than doing the actual thing. 

Need to rewire. Need to connect with reality. Observe my surroundings and not my algorithm. What do people in my area, that have achieved something that I inspire too as well, do? Where do they do it? At what time? 

Most times this "main character energy" makes us so stupid. We think that we are the only ones that have cracked the code, where in reality we are only doing things the wrong way. We could have avoided that if we simply observed.
