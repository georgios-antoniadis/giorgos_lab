I am the sort of person that when life happens I cannot bounce back to default. I tend to get stuck at whatever it is that happened and if there has not been an ultimatum, focusing on what was previously important is real tough. I have not yet figured out the way to combat this, I know however that long term goals are far more important.

Today, being stuck and all, I once again could not force myself to do anything. If it wasn't for having surrounded myself with people who hold me accountable, I am not sure I would even be writing this blog post. It was them that urged me to get out of bed and go on with my day. 

Worthy projects take time, they are not a one-and-done kind of situation. They will span multiple days , weeks or even months in some cases. If you are lucky enough nothing else significant will knock you off course during this time, but that's an exception, not a rule. You have to be consistent, there is no other way around it, so when you get knocked off you need to somehow get back. I will be the first one to admit that without someone or something to hold me accountable I wouldn't have done most of the things that I set out to do. 

By far the most effective means for consistency is "having skin in the game". If you are a student and you do not do your assignments you will not graduate, if you are an employee and don't complete your tasks you'll not get paid, if you have committed to a customer and don't deliver, you are not getting paid and your reputation is on the line. However some goals or projects are not that intense.

For example, if you are trying to lose weight, or if you are developing a local-llm powered QA tool with no customers yet, not finishing them will not result in you not getting paid, not getting a degree or lost reputation. The risk here is that you stay exactly where you were when you first started. Definitely not threatening to your livelihood but will damage your long-term potential in terms of the things that you would like to achieve. 

The most effective way that I have found to staying consistent during such projects is by either having an accountability partner or by surrounding myself with people who pursue the same goal.
